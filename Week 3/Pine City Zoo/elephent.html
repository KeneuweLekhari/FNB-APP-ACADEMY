ELEPHANTS

Elephants are the largest land animals on Earth, easily recognized by their long trunks, large ears, and tusks. They are highly intelligent creatures with strong social bonds and remarkable memories. Elephants live in herds led by a matriarch and communicate using a variety of sounds and gestures. These gentle giants play a vital role in their ecosystems by shaping landscapes and dispersing seeds. Sadly, elephants face threats from habitat loss and poaching, making conservation efforts crucial for their survival.

Elephents are herrbivorous and can be found in different habitats including savannahs, forest, deserts and marshes. They prefer stay near water.
